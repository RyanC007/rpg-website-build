Do you actually own your marketing tools?

Most small business owners assume they own their website, their email list, and their social accounts. A surprising number don't. Find out before it costs you everything.

The Risk Is Real

Losing access to your own business is more common than you think.

A web developer builds your site and registers the domain in their name. An agency manages your Google Analytics and never gives you the login. A contractor sets up your email marketing and leaves. Then they disappear, or you part ways, and suddenly you can't access the tools your business runs on.

We've seen it happen. It's devastating. And it's completely preventable with a 15-minute audit.

The domain is registered in someone else's name.

If you can't log in to your domain registrar, you don't own your domain. Someone else does.

Your website is on a platform you can't control.

If your developer or agency hosts your site and you don't have the login, they can take it down or hold it hostage.

Your email list lives in someone else's account.

Your subscriber list is one of your most valuable business assets. If it's in an account you don't control, it's not really yours.

What We Check

Six areas every small business needs to own.

🌐

Domain Ownership

Is your domain registered in your name, with your email, and with a registrar you can log into? This is the foundation of your online presence.

🖥️

Hosting Access

Do you have login credentials to your hosting account? Can you access your files, databases, and server settings independently?

📧

Email Marketing

Is your email marketing account in your name? Do you have admin access to your subscriber list, automations, and campaign history?

📱

Social Media Accounts

Are you the admin on your own business social accounts? Can you add and remove access without asking someone else?

📊

Analytics and Tracking

Do you have admin access to your Google Analytics, Search Console, and any other tracking tools? Your data belongs to you.

🔧

CRM and Tools

Is your CRM, booking system, or customer database in an account you own and control? These tools hold your customer relationships.
