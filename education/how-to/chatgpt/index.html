How to use ChatGPT in your small business.

ChatGPT is the most widely used AI tool in the world. This guide shows you exactly how to use it for the tasks that save small business owners the most time.

Open ChatGPT Build Your Knowledge Base First

What ChatGPT is good at.

ChatGPT is a conversational AI. You type a message, it responds. The more context you give it, the better the output. Here's where it saves small business owners the most time:

Customer emails

Drafting responses to common customer questions, complaints, and inquiries. Saves 30-60 minutes per day for businesses with high email volume.

Social media captions

Generating on-brand captions for Instagram, LinkedIn, and Facebook. Give it your brand voice guidelines and a topic, and it drafts in seconds.

Blog post outlines

Creating structured outlines for blog posts based on your target keywords. Much faster than starting from a blank page.

FAQ content

Turning your most common customer questions into polished FAQ answers for your website.

Step-by-Step

How to get started in 15 minutes.

1

Create a free account at chat.openai.com

The free version (GPT-3.5) is good for most tasks. The paid version (GPT-4, $20/month) is significantly better for complex writing and analysis. Start free, upgrade when you hit limits.

2

Start with a system prompt

Before you ask ChatGPT to do anything, tell it who you are and what you need. Example: "You are a marketing assistant for [Your Business Name]. We [what you do]. Our brand voice is [describe it]. Our ideal customer is [describe them]."

3

Give it a specific task

The more specific your request, the better the output. Instead of "write a social post," try "write a LinkedIn post for a small business owner about why consistent content marketing matters. Keep it under 150 words. Use a conversational, direct tone."

4

Iterate and refine

ChatGPT is a conversation. If the first output isn't right, tell it what to change. "Make it shorter." "Make it more casual." "Add a specific example." Keep refining until it's right.

5

Save your best prompts

When you find a prompt that works well, save it. Build a library of prompts for your most common tasks. This is how you turn ChatGPT from a one-off tool into a repeatable system.

Common mistakes to avoid.

Mistake: Asking vague questions

"Write me a blog post" will produce generic garbage. "Write a 600-word blog post for small business owners about why they should build an email list before they need it. Use a direct, conversational tone. Include three specific action steps." will produce something usable.

Mistake: Publishing without editing

ChatGPT output is a first draft, not a finished product. Always read it, edit it, and make it sound like you before publishing. AI-generated content that hasn't been edited is obvious and off-putting.

Mistake: Not giving it context

ChatGPT doesn't know your business. Every session starts fresh. Load your Business Backpack at the start of every session so it has the context it needs to produce on-brand output.
