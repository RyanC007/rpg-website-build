SEO, content, social, email. What each one does, what it costs, and what to focus on first when you have limited time and budget.

The honest truth about digital marketing.

Digital marketing is not complicated. It's been made complicated by agencies that benefit from you not understanding it, platforms that want your ad spend, and gurus selling courses about tactics that stopped working two years ago.

The fundamentals haven't changed. Show up where your customers are. Say something useful. Do it consistently. Build trust over time. Everything else is tactics.

Here's what each major channel does and when to use it.

The Channels

What each channel does and when to use it.

SEO (Search Engine Optimization)

Highest Priority

What it does: Gets your website to show up when people search for what you offer on Google. Free traffic that compounds over time.

Time to results: 3-6 months minimum. Slow start, exponential growth.

Cost: Time or agency fees. No per-click cost.

Best for: Every business. Non-negotiable if you want sustainable inbound leads.

Content Marketing

High Priority

What it does: Builds trust and authority by publishing useful content. Blog posts, guides, videos. Each piece is an asset that keeps working.

Time to results: 3-12 months. Works best when paired with SEO.

Cost: Time or content service fees. No distribution cost for organic.

Best for: Businesses where customers research before buying. Service businesses, B2B, professional services.

Email Marketing

High Priority

What it does: Builds a direct relationship with your audience that you own. No algorithm. No platform risk. Highest ROI of any digital channel.

Time to results: Immediate for existing list. Ongoing list building for new subscribers.

Cost: Low. Most email tools are free up to 1,000-2,000 subscribers.

Best for: Every business. Start building your list from day one.

Social Media

Medium Priority

What it does: Builds brand awareness and community. Good for discovery and staying top of mind with existing audience.

Time to results: Variable. Organic reach has declined significantly on most platforms.

Cost: Time. Paid social can be effective but requires budget and testing.

Best for: Businesses with visual products, strong community angle, or where customers spend significant time on social.

Paid Advertising (Google, Meta)

Situational

What it does: Buys traffic and leads immediately. Works as long as you're paying. Stops the moment you stop.

Time to results: Immediate, but requires testing to optimize.

Cost: High. Requires ongoing budget and management.

Best for: Businesses with proven offer and budget to test. Not the right starting point for most small businesses.

Where to start if you have limited time.

If you can only do three things, do these:

1

Optimize your Google Business Profile

Free. Takes 2 hours. Immediately improves your local search visibility. Add photos, complete every field, and start collecting reviews.

2

Start an email list

Sign up for Kit (free up to 10,000 subscribers). Add a signup form to your website. Start sending a monthly newsletter. This is the most valuable marketing asset you can build.

3

Publish one blog post per week

Answer the questions your customers ask most. Use Google's "People also ask" to find topics. Publish consistently. This is the foundation of your SEO strategy.
