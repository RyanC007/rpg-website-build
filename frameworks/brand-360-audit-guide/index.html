What is a Brand 360 Audit?

A Brand 360 Audit is a structured review of your brand across every place a customer might encounter it. Website, social media, Google Business Profile, email, in-person experience, sales materials. All of it.

Most businesses only look at one or two of these at a time. The 360 approach finds the gaps that individual reviews miss. It's the difference between fixing symptoms and fixing the actual problem.

We run this audit at the start of every client engagement. It's also the foundation of our AI Brand Audit tool. This guide walks you through how to do it yourself.

The Five Areas

What the audit covers.

1

Website and Digital Presence

Review your homepage, service pages, about page, and contact page. Check for clarity, load speed, mobile experience, and whether a visitor can understand what you do in under 10 seconds.

Key questions: Is your value proposition clear above the fold? Are your CTAs specific and visible? Is your site mobile-friendly?

2

Brand Voice and Messaging

Review your messaging across all channels. Does it sound consistent? Does it speak to your ideal customer? Is it clear about what you do and who you serve?

Key questions: Would your ideal customer recognize themselves in your messaging? Does your brand sound like one person or three?

3

SEO and Search Visibility

Review your keyword rankings, meta titles, meta descriptions, heading structure, and Google Business Profile. Find out if you're showing up when your customers search for what you offer.

Key questions: What keywords are you ranking for? What keywords should you be ranking for? Is your Google Business Profile complete and accurate?

4

Social Media Presence

Review your social profiles, posting frequency, content mix, and engagement. Check whether your social presence is building trust or just filling space.

Key questions: Are your profiles complete and consistent? Is your content mix balanced between value and promotion? Are you posting consistently?

5

Competitive Position

Review your top 3-5 competitors. What are they doing well? Where are the gaps you can own? What keywords are they ranking for that you're not?

Key questions: What makes you different from your competitors? Are you communicating that difference clearly? Where are the content gaps you can fill?

How to use this framework.

The Brand 360 Audit is designed to be done in a focused 2-3 hour session. Here's how we recommend approaching it:

1

Block the time

Set aside 2-3 hours with no interruptions. This is strategic work. It deserves focused attention.

2

Use the template

The full template (available to paid members) walks you through each section with specific questions and scoring criteria. Work through each area systematically.

3

Score each area

Rate each area on a scale of 1-5. Be honest. The audit only helps if you're truthful about where you actually are.

4

Prioritize the fixes

You'll find more issues than you can fix at once. Prioritize by impact. Fix the things that are actively costing you customers first.
