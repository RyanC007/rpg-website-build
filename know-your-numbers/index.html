The Numbers That Matter

Four metrics every small business owner needs to understand.

You don't need an MBA. You need to understand four numbers. Here's what they are and why they matter.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

How much does it cost you to get one new customer? Add up everything you spend on marketing, ads, sales, and time, then divide by the number of new customers you got. If your CAC is higher than what a customer pays you, you're losing money on every sale.

Formula: Total Marketing Spend / New Customers Acquired

Lifetime Value (LTV)

How much does a customer spend with you over the entire time they're a customer? LTV tells you how much you can afford to spend to acquire a customer and still be profitable. A high LTV means you can outspend competitors on acquisition.

Formula: Average Purchase Value x Purchase Frequency x Customer Lifespan

Pricing Profitability

Are your prices actually covering your costs and leaving you with a profit? Many small businesses underprice their services because they're afraid to charge what they're worth. We help you figure out what you should be charging.

Revenue - All Costs (including your time) = Real Profit

Break-Even Point

How many customers or sales do you need each month to cover your costs? Your break-even number is the floor. Everything above it is profit. Knowing this number changes how you make decisions about spending and growth.

Fixed Costs / (Price - Variable Cost per Unit) = Break-Even Units

Why It Matters

You're investing in marketing. Make sure your business model can support the growth.

Content marketing and SEO work. But if your pricing isn't right, or your CAC is higher than your LTV, more customers just means more losses. Before you invest in visibility, make sure your numbers work.
